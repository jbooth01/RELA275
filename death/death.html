Death

His Death

Jesus' suffering didn't stop in the Garden of Gethsemane, but it continued on after when he was scourged and then crucified at Calvary, where he was raised up on the cross and slain for the sins of the world. (1 Nephi 11:33)


One of the consequences of the fall of Adam and Eve was that death was brought into the world. In order for Christ to overcome physical death, he himself had to die. Even though he was the son of God and had the power to stop the people from crucifying him, Jesus submitted to the will of the father and allowed himself to be killed. (3 Nephi 27:13-14)
